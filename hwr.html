Heavy water reactors are very similar to there light water counterparts apart from the fact that they use heavy water. The reactor uses heavy water as both coolant and moderator much in the same fashion as Pressurised Light Water reactors.

Heavy Water (Deuterium):

Heavy water gets its name from the fact that it is heavier than normal water. The reason it is heavier is because unlike normal water which is made up of Hydrogen and Oxygen, heavy water is made up of Deuterium and oxygen. Deuterium is an isotope of hydrogen with one extra neutron than normal. Its main difference is that it absorbs less neutrons than normal hydrogen meaning more neutrons are available for fission.


Pros:

  • Heavy water reactors can work with many fuels including un-enriched natural uranium. This is a huge benefit as it reduces costs dramatically for fuel.
  • They also can be refuelled during operation meaning that they can continuously operate.

Cons:

  • They produce Tritium which is radioactive and know to leak out in small quantities.
  • They can be modified to produce more weapons grade plutonium than normal reactors would.
