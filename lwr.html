Pressurised water reactors are the most common nuclear reactors. They use water as both a coolant and a moderator however the coolant water is separated from the water that is turned into steam and heats it in a heat exchange.

Pros:
  • The secondary loop for water to be turned into steam makes maintenance easier as radiation is contained to the core reactor.
  • The pressurised water reactor has a compact design which makes it perfect for naval applications and has been used on aircraft carriers.
  • The design has been around for a long time meaning that it has a good safety record and it can be optimised to get the most power output.

Cons:
  • The reactor design does not allow the breeding of new isotopes and elements meaning that it cannot create new fuel.
  • Coolant is kept at high pressure meaning that if there is a leak lots of it can escape quickly meaning that a lot of investment needs to be made into redundant coolant systems.

Boiling water reactors are the second most common reactors and use water as both a coolant and moderator. There main deviation from pressurised water reactors is there lack of secondary loop. They do not have a heat transfer between the coolant and water from elsewhere. Instead coolant is converted into steam and sent from the reactor straight to the turbine.

  • The main advantage to this design is that it has a much simpler design as there is no need for a heat transfer simplifying the implementation of the design.
